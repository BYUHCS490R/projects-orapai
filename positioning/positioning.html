What is normal flow in HTML?

Normal flow in HTML is the natural way elements are arranged on a webpage, from top to bottom and left to right. It’s how the browser positions content before any special styling or layout changes are added.

What does float do? Why do you need to clear the float?

The float property moves an element to the left or right of its container so other elements can wrap around it. You need to clear the float so the next elements don’t accidentally overlap or wrap around the floated item.

What do absolute, fixed, and relative do?

Absolute, fixed, and relative are position properties that control where elements appear. Relative moves an element slightly from its normal spot, absolute positions it within its nearest positioned parent, and fixed keeps it in place even when scrolling.
