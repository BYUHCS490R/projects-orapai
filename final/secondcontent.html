Places in Spain

Cape Finisterre (Cabo Fisterra) in Galicia

The "end of the world" in Spain refers to Cape Finisterre (Cabo Fisterra) in Galicia, historically considered the westernmost point of the Iberian Peninsula. The name comes from the Latin finis terrae, meaning "end of the earth". The site was significant in ancient and Roman times as a place where they believed the sun set into the sea, and today it is the final destination for many pilgrims completing the Camino de Santiago.
